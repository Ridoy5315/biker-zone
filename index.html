Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

If helmet shows visible wear like cracks, fading, or loose padding, it's time for a replacement.Helmets should be replaced every 5-7 years, or sooner if they’ve been involved in an impact, even if they look fine.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

After storage, check your bike’s tires, brakes, and fluids for any signs of wear or damage before riding. It’s essential to inspect and possibly service your bike after long-term storage to ensure it’s safe and road-ready.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Always wear protective gear, obey traffic laws, and stay visible to other drivers for a safe ride. Ride defensively, maintain a safe distance, and be mindful of road conditions to prevent accidents.
